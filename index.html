Who is eligible to apply

Eligibility for a Saudi visa from Dubai

If you're living in Dubai, the visa application rules differ for citizens and residents.

  • UAE citizens and passport holders can directly receive a Saudi visa upon arrival, without needing to apply in advance.
  • UAE residents (non-citizens) must apply for a Saudi visa in advance, based on their nationality
  • UAE residents can also obtain a Saudi multiple-entry visa, valid for multiple entries and exits within a year.
  • The Saudi eVisa, a recently introduced tourist visa for Saudi Arabia, is available to citizens of 63 countries.
  • If you have a short-term visa in the UAE, such as a visitor visa, you can still go to Saudi Arabia with a separate KSA visa.
  • Specific documents are needed to obtain a Saudi visa.

Visa FAQs: Get Clear Answers

  • Is a Saudi visa available for UAE residents?

    Yes, foreign nationals living in the UAE require a Saudi visa.. UAE citizens don’t need a visa unless traveling for religious purposes.

  • Can a UAE Emirates ID get me into Saudi Arabia

    To enter Saudi Arabia, residents of GCC nations or citizens of the United Arab Emirates do not require a visa. Other UAE citizens are unable to utilize their UAE Emirates ID and must apply for a Saudi visa.

  • Can I get a Saudi visa on arrival?

    GCC nationals can obtain a Saudi visa upon arrival.

  • What kinds of Saudi Arabia visas are there?

    Depending on the reason for your trip, Saudi Arabia offers a variety of visa categories, such as business, tourist, work, and religious pilgrimage (Hajj and Umrah) visas.

  • Can I extend my Saudi visa?

    Yes, you can extend your Saudi visa, but be sure to review the specific regulations for your visa type.
